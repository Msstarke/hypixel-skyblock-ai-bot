# Skills

The skills menu
There are twelve Skills in Hypixel SkyBlock, which are automatically leveled up based on the actions performed by a player.
In order to gain XP for a specific Skill , players need to perform tasks corresponding to a Skill . For example, to level up Mining , the player would need to mine blocks.  Players can also level up their Skills by collecting resources from their Minions for a reduced amount of XP.
Leveling up Skills provides rewards and also permanently increases various player Stats , as well as SkyBlock XP . For example, leveling up Farming gives the player coins and increases their ❤ Health .
The player's Skill level can be seen by opening the SkyBlock Menu and going to "Skills" (displayed as a Diamond Sword ).
This also displays average Skill level, across all skills except Social , Runecrafting and Dungeoneering .

# Farming

Farming is one of the Skills available for players to level up as they perform certain actions that give them Farming XP.
Roman Numerals are used to express its levels in-game.
Although Farming goes from levels 1–50 (I – L) by default, there is +50 Farming XP required to reach the first level, meaning there is a "level 0", just like all other Skills .
The Farming Level Cap can be increased through Jacob's Farming Contests , to a maximum of Level 60 (LX). In order to reach this cap, players must place in the GOLD bracket of a unique crop type, then purchase the upgrade from Anita in the ⏣ Farmhouse . This can be done 10 times, increasing the cap one level for each unique crop type mastered.

## Mechanics

### Public Islands
Farming on Public Islands, specifically the Hub Island (in ⏣ Village , ⏣ Mountain and ⏣ Farm ), Farming Islands ( ⏣ The Barn and ⏣ Mushroom Desert , on the later of which are Glowing Mushrooms ) and Crimson Isle (featuring Nether Wart as well as the unique crops of Mushroom Spores and Red Thornleaf which require Spore Harvester and Thornleaf Scythe respectively to be collected) serves mostly as an introduction to the skill, and the means to unlock their respective collection. More focused or thorough Farming is done better on Garden , if the crops allow it.
Inside the Rift Dimension players can also find the unique crops of Riftwart Roots , Agaricus Cap , Caducous Stem , Half-Eaten Carrot and Wilted Berberis , which can only be harvested using the Wand of Farming . Those crops cannot be planted on Garden, but fortunately, comparatively fewer of them are required.
The Farming Islands also feature Animals, which grant Farming XP when slain, and have their own farming collections. Those cannot be grinded efficiently, and as such Minions are recommended. Although Animal Axes do exist, for those who wish to finish their collections manually.

### Private Islands
Before the release of Garden , players would build Farms on their own islands, as it was significantly more efficient than Farming on public islands, whether it was for Collection , Farming XP or coins . The addition of Garden made the creation of Farms much easier and farming itself much more efficient.
Once it has been unlocked — which requires SkyBlock Level 5 and is then accessed via Sam who appears on player's Private Island — Farming on The Garden is the only viable way to Farm, at Public islands frequently have multiple people and don't allow for custom farms; and on Private Island , player's ☘ Farming Fortune is disabled. Unlocking plots on Garden requires Compost , obtained from Composter and before being able to use unlocked plots, players first need to clean them. This can be done with vanilla tools or via Sam's Scythe , Garden Scythe or Plot Eraser .
While Garden has presets for each of the crops, there are farms that can be built manually that are more efficient, whether it's because of their use of the space or by allowing the max number of Crops broken per second. Players can build Farms using tools such as Builder's Wand , Builder's Ruler , InfiniDirt™ Wand , Magical Water Bucket and Block Zapper , as well as vanilla tools enchanted with Efficiency .
Crops, similarly to vanilla Minecraft, require Water to grow, they however don't require a light source. Crops each require a different Substrate , such as Tilted Dirt for Wheat or Carrots , or Soul Sand for Nether Wart . Dirt can be tilted en masse via Hoe of Great Tilling , Hoe of Greater Tilling and Hoe of Greatest Tilling . Crops can be planted manually or using Basket of Seeds and Nether Wart Pouch . Red and Brown Mushrooms however cannot be planted using these items and can only be done manually. They however spread naturally, so it doesn't complicate things too mcuh.
To prevent having to replant crops upon every harvest, players can use the Replenish enchantment, obtained from Cocoa Beans VIII . Players can also use Delicate , obtained from Anita , which prevents the breaking of Pumpkin and Melon stems, as well as crops that are not fully grown.

#### Pests
By reaching Garden 5 , players unlock a new unique mechanic — Pests. When farming on ⏣ The Garden , one of 13 (one for every crop) pests may spawn. The same happens once for every hour of the Garden being unloaded. Up to 8 can be spawned at the same time, and the more of them there are, the more is player's ☘ Farming Fortune reduced. Players can use Pest Repellent or Pest Repellent MAX to slow their spawning, but it is recommended to instead kill them using Vacuums , as each pest drops Crops as well as unique items which can help players farm. Additionally, by slaying Pests players obtain a special currency — Pests — which can be traded with Pesthunter Phillip for Pest-related items.

#### Greenhouse
The Greenhouse is a plot in The Garden unlocked at Garden 7 after a short quest. Crops planted inside take longer to grow, but in return also grant increased drops. Additionally, meeting certain conditions can lead to the creation of Mutations .

### Farming XP Boosts
Players can increase their farming XP gained by increasing ☯ Farming Wisdom , which can be increased by:
- Booster Cookie : 25 ☯ Farming Wisdom
- Drinking Farming XP Boost potions: 5–20 ☯ Farming Wisdom . God Potions include the Farming XP Boost III effect, and has significantly higher duration.
- God Potions include the Farming XP Boost III effect, and has significantly higher duration.
- Rabbit Pet : When at [ Lv100 ] , the RARE Rabbit Pet grants 25 ☯ Farming Wisdom while the EPIC , LEGENDARY , and MYTHIC variants grant 30 ☯ Farming Wisdom .
- Farming Tools give 1–3 ☯ Farming Wisdom . They can also be upgraded with the Blessed reforge and the Cultivating Enchantment. A maxed Farming tool can grant 18 ☯ Farming Wisdom , which can be increased by 5% if the tool is an Axe, via the Forest Essence Shop .
- The Dragonfly Shard 's Garden Wisdom attribute grants 0.5–5 ☯ Farming Wisdom , which can be increased 20% with the Echo of Wisdom , which can be upgraded by 50% with Echo of Echoes to a total of 6.5 ☯ Farming Wisdom .
- The Fruit Bowl consumable can be used to gain 1 ☯ Farming Wisdom .

## Spelunker
Spelunker is an ability unlocked exclusively by leveling up the Mining Skill. Each level of Spelunker grants +4 ☘ Mining Fortune for almost any Mining XP-yielding block to drop 2-4x its original amount (2x Ore Drop at levels 1-25, 3x at levels 26–50, 4x at levels 51–60).
Each level of Mining grants one level of Spelunker. For example, if the player is on Mining level 15, they would be on Spelunker level 15, and the chance for x2 Ore Drop would be 60%.
The Spelunker perk does not apply to Redstone or Lapis Lazuli and does not influence how much ore is gained from mining it. This is believed to be a bug as much more Redstone and Lapis Lazuli is gained per ore than the others.

# Combat

Combat is one of the core player Skills in SkyBlock. It can be levelled up by killing mobs, claiming Combat Minion drops, claiming Bestiary milestones, and a multitude of other methods. The maximum level Combat can go up to is Combat LX .

## Overview
Combat XP is gained from killing mobs: each mob grants a set amount of XP when killed, with the exception of mobs spawned on the Private Island , which do not grant XP on kill.
The amount of Combat XP gained on mob kill increases with ☯ Combat Wisdom . ☯ Combat Wisdom can be increased through numerous different methods, such as using different weapons and armor that grant ☯ Combat Wisdom , equipping Pets that grant ☯ Combat Wisdom , or syphoning the Apex Dragon Shard .

# Fishing

Fishing is one of the core player Skills in SkyBlock. It can be levelled up by catching items and treasure, as well as by killing Sea Creatures .
Leveling up the Fishing Skill will give the player:
- ❤ Health

## Fishing
The player can fish by casting a Fishing Rod into Water or Lava . After an amount of time, they will catch an item or Sea Creature .
The player can also fish on Dirt using a Dirt Rod , however it should be noted that this is merely a gimmick and not a meaningful part of fishing progression.

## Sea Creatures
When fishing, players have a chance to catch a Sea Creature . Sea Creatures are mobs which drop Fishing loot and Fishing XP when killed. The player's α Sea Creature Chance determines the chance they have to catch a Sea Creature and ⚓ Double Hook Chance determines the chance of fishing two Sea Creatures at once.
As a player increases their fishing level, they unlock stronger creatures that give more XP and better loot. Killing sea creatures on the private island does not provide fishing XP.
Sea Creatures cannot be damaged with arrows directly, except those shot from Prismarine Bow .
Runic sea creatures have a 50% chance to drop a Wake Rune upon death.

## Drops
Items can be obtained from fishing if the player does not catch a Sea Creature, depending on their ⛃ Treasure Chance .
There is a base chance of 89% for a GOOD CATCH , 10% for a GREAT CATCH , and 1% for an OUTSTANDING CATCH . The chance for great and outstanding catches can be increased using Blessed Bait , the Blessing enchantment, and a RARE or better Hermit Crab Pet .

### Location
Certain drops have a higher chance to be or can only be fished up in specific Locations . In certain locations, there is a 25% chance for the drop obtained to be rolled from a loot pool unique to the pond.

## Tips
There are two main reasons to fish, for the XP and for valuable drops. No matter which is being gone for, it is required to have as much ☂ Fishing Speed and α Sea Creature Chance as possible. When fishing for XP, getting as much ☯ Fishing Wisdom as possible is recommended. When fishing for valuable drops (from Sea Creatures), getting as much ✯ Magic Find as possible is recommended. If possible, use the Whale Bait, which is currently the best bait. If it is too expensive, Glowy Chum Bait or Fish Bait serve well as a cheaper alternative.
The current best Water Fishing Rod progression is the following:
- Fishing 0-4: Prismarine Rod
- Fishing 5-9: Chum Rod
- Fishing 10-14: Challenging Rod
- Fishing 15-19: Rod of Champions
- Fishing 20-23: Rod of Legends
- Fishing 24-50: Rod of the Sea
The current best Lava Fishing Rod progression is the following:
- Fishing 27-29: Magma Rod
- Fishing 30-34: Inferno Rod
- Fishing 35-50: Hellfire Rod
The current best Fishing Armor progression is the following:
- Fishing 0-12: 3/4 Angler Armor and Water Hydra Head
- Fishing 13-16: 3/4 Salmon Armor and Water Hydra Head
- Fishing 17-19: Sponge Armor
- Fishing 20-23: Diver's Armor
- Fishing 24-26: Shark Scale Armor
- Fishing 27: 3/4 Shark Scale Armor and Slug Boots
- Fishing 28-32: 2/4 Shark Scale Armor, Moogma Leggings and Slug Boots

- Fishing 35: Lava Sea Creature Armor

- Fishing 45-50: Magma Lord Armor

## Trivia
- There are currently 5 Fairy Souls that can only be obtained by fishing. Two of them can only be fished up in the Fairy Grotto .
- The Fishing Crystal that spawns with the pond island grants more frequent drops of Prismarine Shards and Prismarine Crystals .
- Daytime occurs between 6:00 am–7:00 pm whilst Night time occurs between 7:00 pm–6:00 a.m. It is also indicated by the sun or moon next to the time in the sidebar.
- There are two blocks on the Fairy Lake bed where there is fire underneath; however, it doesn't seem to serve any purpose at the moment.
- The Combat XP buffs from Slayer , Combat XP Boost Potion , Viking's Tear , or Accessories like Hunter Ring apply to Fishing XP earned from killing sea creatures.
- XP from Sea Creatures based on Zombies ( Sea Walker , Monster of the Deep , Water Hydra ) count for Zombie Slayer . Similarly, Lava Flames , the only Blaze Sea Creature, count for Blaze Slayer .
- The chicken of the Monster of the Deep drops more XP. But for a Runic Monster of the Deep only killing the Zombie yields a rune.
- Spooky fishing mobs can be caught whenever the Fear Mongerer is in the hub, not just during the spooky festival.
- Lava Fishing could be a reference to Terraria's lava fishing mechanic.
- The base coin yield from treasures before scaling with the fishing level is 1,000-21,000 in water and 500-10,500 in lava.
- The chance to get coins from treasures is estimated to be 31.8%±2.3%.

# Enchanting

Enchanting is one of the Skills available for players to level up as they perform certain actions that give them Enchanting XP.
Roman Numerals are used to express its level in-game.

## Leveling XP
Enchanting items in an Enchantment Table or Anvil grants Enchanting XP relative to the number of levels spent. The amount of Enchanting XP gained from spending X {\displaystyle X} levels is given by X P = 3.5 X 3 2 {\displaystyle XP = 3.5X^\frac{3}{2}} .
It is also possible to gain Enchanting XP from fishing up Experience Bottles , Grand Experience Bottles or Titanic Experience Bottles .
Additionally, the Experimentation Table can be used to gain significant amounts of Enchanting XP.

# Taming

Taming is one of the Skills available for players to level up as they perform certain actions that give them Taming XP. Leveling Taming increases the player's Extra Pet XP , Exp Share rate , and ♣ Pet Luck .
Roman Numerals are used to express its levels in-game.
Although Taming goes from levels 1 - 60 (I - LX), there is 50 XP required to reach the first level, meaning there is a "level 0", just like all other skills.
The Taming Level Cap can be increased through George , to a maximum of Level 60 (LX). In order to reach this cap, players have to give George specific Pets (with the same or higher rarity):
- EPIC Rift Ferret Pet
- EPIC Slug Pet
- EPIC Spirit Pet
- EPIC Giraffe Pet
- EPIC Jellyfish Pet
- EPIC Bal Pet
- EPIC Baby Yeti Pet
- LEGENDARY Black Cat Pet
- RARE Frost Wisp Pet
- MYTHIC Enderman Pet
The Pets will not be given back to you, so it's recommended to give the level 1 pets to George (or whatever is cheapest).

## Leveling XP
The amount of Taming XP gained depends on the Skill XP gained and on the Pet's type. Using a Pet whose type differs from the skill XP gained results in less Taming XP earned. A pet must be active to earn Taming XP. Different skills result in different amounts of Taming XP gained.

## Zoologist
Zoologist is an ability exclusively unlocked by leveling up the Taming Skill. Each level of Zoologist grants +1% Pet XP gained and +1 ♣ Pet Luck .
Each level of Taming grants one level of Zoologist. For example, if the player were on Taming level 15, the player would therefore also be on Zoologist level 15.

## Leveling Rewards
The cumulative rewards at Taming level XXV (25) are:
- +25% extra Pet XP
- +25 ♣ Pet Luck

- +50% extra Pet XP
- +50 ♣ Pet Luck

## Uses
Leveling up Taming is not only good for extra pet luck but can also be used in order to upgrade pets using Kat. Players will need taming level 10 to get a pet from Rare to Epic, taming level 20 in order to get a pet from Epic to Legendary, and taming level 25 to get a pet from Legendary to Mythic. It's also very effective for leveling pets faster if the player has a decent level of at least 25. It can also be used to upgrade the base damage of the Daedalus Axe .

## Trivia
- Doing an action with a pet that is already level 100 still yields Taming XP. The excess of XP will be "credited" to the pet, so if its rarity is upgraded, the excess will be added to the pet's XP. This means that the player's pet can still be level 100 after picking it up from Kat, if it has enough excessive XP at the previous rarity. However, you cannot feed a level 100 pet Pet Food .

# Carpentry

Carpentry Table
Carpentry is one of the Skills available for players to level up from Crafting items. It allows the player to craft and use Carpentry items using the Carpentry Table .
The skill is unlocked after giving the Carpenter 64x Wool , who will then reward the player with the recipe for the Carpentry Table .
At Carpentry III (3) , the player unlocks Quick Crafting , which allows them to instantly craft an item, as long as they have the required materials in their inventory. However, it is only available to players with the 2 [VIP] rank and above. On Ⓑ Bingo profiles, it is available to all players, including those without a rank. On ☀ Stranded profiles, it is only available to 3 [ VIP + ] players and above.

## Furniture
Carpentry is the main way to obtain furniture and is the main purpose of carpentry. The player can craft Furniture using the Carpentry Table .

## Leveling Up
The player can level up Carpentry by crafting items. The XP gained is 3% of the combined NPC sell price of the ingredients used to craft the item. Most vanilla recipes do not grant Carpentry XP.

## Leveling Rewards
The cumulative rewards at Carpentry level 25 are:
- 25 ❤ Health
- 1,334,100 coins
- 200 SkyBlock XP
- Access to various Furniture Recipes.
The cumulative rewards at Carpentry level 50 are:

- 17,259,100 coins
- 700 SkyBlock XP

# Runecrafting

Runecrafting is one of the Skills available for players to level up by fusing runes, killing Runic Mobs , or by killing Bosses . It allows the player to fuse Runes onto their weapons and armor. Runecrafting is a cosmetic-only mechanic, and can only be leveled up to level 3 with the 1 Default rank.
Roman Numerals are used to express its levels in-game.

## Mechanics
Runecrafting is a cosmetic skill that has content locked to players without a Rank . Players with no ranks cannot increase past level 3 in the skill , and higher ranks apply a higher exp multiplier. As a "cosmetic" skill no important content or any stats require it to be leveled, and other than some small benefits it may provide (such as tabbed chests) it's purely decoration. The other cosmetic skill is Social .
Each rune has an associated level. The runes can be fused- however, they cannot be activated until the player has their Runecrafting level equal to or greater than the level of the rune.

### Runic Mobs
Runes are obtained from Runic Mobs. Runic Mobs are normal SkyBlock Mobs , but with 4x higher ❤ Health , and deal double damage. They have a purple name tag, and a much lower chance to spawn. When killed, they are guaranteed to drop a Rune .
All mobs can be Runic except Ender Dragon , Magma Cube Boss , Slayer Boss/Miniboss, Dungeon Boss or Dungeon Boss Minion .
Certain runes are also obtained from Slayers or as special rewards.

### Fusing Runes
Runes can be fused on the Runic Pedestal , found behind the Blacksmith and owned by the NPC Dusk .
Fusing two runes together will have a chance of upgrading the rune to the next level, up to the maximum level of 3. Upon success or failure, the second rune is consumed. If this process does not succeed, the remaining rune will not gain the level. The chance of success can be seen in the Runic Pedestal while hovering over the apply trigger. Regardless of the outcome, this will also grant Runecrafting experience. More experience is awarded upon success.
Runes can be fused onto Swords , Bows , Chestplates , and Boots , depending on the type of rune. The player must have their Runecrafting level either equal to or greater than the level of the rune, or they won't be able to fuse it. This only applies to fuse runes to items; runes above the player's Runecrafting level can still be combined together. Fusing runes onto items will also grant Runecrafting experience.
