# Dungeon Floor Guide

Comprehensive floor-by-floor guide for The Catacombs dungeon in Hypixel SkyBlock, covering normal mode (F1-F7) and Master Mode (M1-M7).

## Dungeon Progression Path

The recommended order for dungeon progression:
1. Reach Combat Level 15, enter the Dungeon Hub, talk to Mort
2. Run Entrance floors to learn basic mechanics and get some Catacombs XP
3. Progress F1 -> F2 -> F3 to unlock solo play (Saul's Recommendation from completing F3)
4. Grind F4/F5 for mid-game gear (Spirit items, Shadow Assassin armor, Livid Dagger)
5. Grind F6 for Giant's Sword and Necromancer Lord armor
6. Grind F7 for endgame wither armor and weapons (Necron, Hyperion, etc.)
7. Enter Master Mode starting M1 once you have strong F7 gear
8. Progress M1 -> M7 for master stars and endgame loot

## Scoring System

Dungeon score is made up of four categories:
- **Skill Score**: Based on deaths and failed puzzles. Fewer deaths and puzzle failures = higher score.
- **Exploration Score**: Based on percentage of rooms completed and percentage of secrets found.
- **Speed Score**: Based on time taken to complete the run. Faster = higher score.
- **Bonus Score**: Varies by floor and active modifiers (Paul mayor perk, etc.)

### Grade Thresholds
| Score | Grade |
|-------|-------|
| 0-99 | D |
| 100-159 | C |
| 160-229 | B |
| 230-269 | A |
| 270-299 | S |
| 300+ | S+ |

### Tips for S+ Runs
- Find all secrets (or at least the required percentage)
- Complete all rooms
- Zero deaths, zero failed puzzles
- Fast clear time
- S+ on Floor V+ unlocks the Bedrock chest (best loot tier)

## Reward Chests

Six chest tiers with escalating costs:
- **Wood Chest**: Free (always available)
- **Gold Chest**: 25k-100k coins
- **Diamond Chest**: 50k-250k coins
- **Emerald Chest**: 100k-500k coins (requires A rank)
- **Obsidian Chest**: 250k-1M coins (requires S rank)
- **Bedrock Chest**: 2M coins (requires S+ rank, Floors V-VII only)

Players must reach their 2nd Class Milestone to open chests. Gear from chests can randomly receive star enhancements, Recombobulation, or bonus enchants.

## Secrets

Secrets are hidden items found in dungeon rooms at fixed locations. Types include:
- **Item Secrets**: Hidden behind paintings, inside chests, or in destructible blocks. Give items like Superboom TNT, potions, or Dungeon loot.
- **Bat Secrets**: Bats that spawn when you get close. Kill them to collect the secret.
- **Wither Essence Secrets**: Found in specific locations, give Wither Essence.
- **Redstone Secrets**: Activated by stepping on pressure plates, clicking buttons, or triggering redstone mechanisms.

Secrets contribute heavily to the Exploration score. Finding a higher percentage of secrets dramatically improves your grade. Many rooms have well-known secret locations that experienced players memorize.

## Class Recommendations Per Floor

### Early Floors (F1-F3)
- Any class works, these floors are forgiving
- Healer is helpful for learning groups
- Mage with Bonzo Staff or Frozen Scythe is effective

### Mid Floors (F4-F5)
- **Archer** starts becoming very strong with Juju Shortbow
- **Berserker** is good with Livid Dagger or Shadow Fury
- **Tank** is important for F5+ to survive Livid clones
- **Healer** remains valuable for less experienced groups

### Late Floors (F6-F7)
- **Archer** is the best DPS class, especially with Terminator bow
- **Berserker** strong with Hyperion for clearing rooms
- **Mage** strong with Hyperion for room clearing and left-click Mage Staff damage
- **Tank** essential for F7 to survive Necron's attacks and protect the team
- **Healer** useful but often replaced by a second DPS in experienced groups

### Ideal F7 Party Composition
- 1 Archer (Terminator) - main boss DPS
- 1 Berserker or Mage (Hyperion) - room clear
- 1 Tank (Goldor armor) - survivability
- 1 Healer or second DPS - support or extra damage
- Flex spot based on team needs

## Floor Entrance (The Catacombs - Entrance)

- **Requirement**: Combat Level 15
- **Boss**: None (no boss fight)
- **Dungeon Size**: Tiny (4x4)
- **Purpose**: Tutorial floor for learning dungeon mechanics
- **Notable**: Players auto-revive after 15 seconds. Good for learning room layouts, secret locations, and basic dungeon mechanics.
- **Loot**: Basic dungeon gear, small amounts of essence

## Floor 1 (F1) - Bonzo

- **Requirement**: Catacombs Level 1
- **Boss**: Bonzo
- **Dungeon Size**: Small

### Boss Mechanics
**Phase 1**: Bonzo summons Undead mobs (50k HP each) while firing Wither Skulls (8,000 damage). He holds a Dark Orb and moves around the arena.

**Phase 2**: After being defeated once, Bonzo revives at full health with new attacks. He becomes more aggressive with melee strikes and Balloon attacks:
- Bonzo's Balloon: Single projectile dealing 8,000 damage
- Balloon Barrage: Multi-directional balloon assault (hide behind pillars to avoid)

### Arena
Seven short pillars ring an open center (circus theme). Use pillars as cover from Balloon Barrage.

### Key Strategies
- Stay behind pillars during Balloon Barrage
- Kill Undead summons to reduce incoming damage
- Phase 2 is the real fight; save cooldowns for it

### Notable Loot
- Bonzo's Mask (decent early game helmet, saves you from death once)
- Bonzo's Staff (early mage weapon)
- Red Nose

### Recommended Gear
- Any Dungeon-converted armor (Zombie Soldier, etc.)
- Rogue Sword or basic dungeon weapons
- Very low gear requirements, almost anything works

## Floor 2 (F2) - Scarf

- **Requirement**: Catacombs Level 3
- **Boss**: Scarf
- **Dungeon Size**: Small
- **Boss HP**: 1M (Normal)

### Boss Mechanics
**Phase 1**: Scarf is protected by a barrier. Players must defeat four Undead minions first:
- Undead Warrior (Berserk-type)
- Undead Priest (Healer-type) - KILL THIS FIRST
- Undead Mage
- Undead Archer

**Phase 2**: Barrier drops, all Undead revive and become stronger. Fight Scarf alongside his minions.
- Skull Barrage: Launches explosive wither skulls in a wide arc
- Soul Sand Blizzard: Deals true damage based on max HP, applies slowness

### Key Strategies
- Always kill the Undead Priest first in Phase 2, as it heals Scarf and other minions
- Focus Scarf once the Priest is dead
- Stay spread out to minimize Skull Barrage damage

### Notable Loot
- Scarf's Studies
- Adaptive Blade (decent early weapon)
- Red Scarf

### Recommended Gear
- Zombie Soldier Armor or better
- Any dungeon weapon with decent damage

## Floor 3 (F3) - The Professor

- **Requirement**: Catacombs Level 5
- **Boss**: The Professor
- **Dungeon Size**: Medium
- **Boss HP**: 3M (Normal), deals 6,640 damage
- **Special**: Completing F3 lets you claim Saul's Recommendation for solo dungeon access

### Boss Mechanics
**Phase 1**: The Professor is shielded. Must defeat 5 Guardians simultaneously (they revive every 10 seconds at 80% HP):
- Healthy Guardian: Uses "Giga Heal" to restore all Guardians (interrupt with 10+ rapid hits)
- Chaos Guardian: Has an anger meter; proximity causes lightning damage
- Laser Guardian: Charges a focus beam for ~12 seconds dealing sustained damage
- Reinforced Guardian: Summons TNT and Rogue Guardians
- Rogue Guardian: Spawns throughout the fight

**Phase 2**: All Guardians down, Professor becomes vulnerable with 3M HP. Attacks with TNT Rain and Prismarine Blizzard.

**Phase 3**: At 25% HP, Professor absorbs Guardians and transforms. Arena floods, boats spawn.

### Key Strategies
- Coordinate to kill all Guardians at roughly the same time (they revive quickly)
- Interrupt Healthy Guardian's Giga Heal with rapid hits
- Stay away from Chaos Guardian unless attacking it
- Dodge Laser Guardian's beam

### Notable Loot
- Suspicious Vial
- Adaptive Armor set (Helmet, Chestplate, Leggings, Boots) - class-changing armor

### Recommended Gear
- Adaptive Armor from F2/F3 chests or Zombie Soldier
- Bonzo Staff for mages, Rogue Sword or Undead Sword for melee

## Floor 4 (F4) - Thorn

- **Requirement**: Catacombs Level 9
- **Boss**: Thorn
- **Dungeon Size**: Medium

### Boss Mechanics
Thorn is a unique ghost-type boss that CANNOT be damaged by normal weapons. Players must use the Spirit Bow.

**Spirit Bear Phase**: Kill 25 Spirit Animals to spawn the Spirit Bear (5M HP). Defeat the Spirit Bear to get the Spirit Bow.

**Thorn Phase**: Use Spirit Bow to shoot Thorn. Takes 4 hits to kill in Normal Mode. Thorn speeds up as he takes more damage and becomes harder to hit near death.

### Spirit Animals
| Animal | HP | Key Mechanic |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| Spirit Bull | 1.5M | Charges at players, deals 10% max HP as true damage |
| Spirit Wolf | 500K | Swarms in groups |
| Spirit Bat | 300K | Targets airborne players, ignores i-frames |
| Spirit Rabbit | 675K | Launches players upward |
| Spirit Chicken | 350K | Strikes with lightning (25% max HP), turns into mines |
| Spirit Sheep | 650K | Explodes like a creeper, up to 30k damage |

### Arena Mechanics
- Green circles heal players
- Orange circles deal fire damage
- Spectators attack with spirit bats if you enter their area

### Key Strategies
- Kill Spirit Animals efficiently to spawn the Spirit Bear quickly
- Focus the Spirit Bear as a team
- Only one Spirit Bow shot per person; coordinate who shoots Thorn
- Avoid Spirit Sheep explosions (highest damage mob)
- Stay out of orange circles

### Notable Loot
- Spirit Bone, Spirit Wing, Spirit Boots, Spirit Sword
- Spirit Pet
- Spirit Bow (usable outside dungeons)

### Recommended Gear
- Adaptive Armor or better
- Strong melee weapon for Spirit Animals
- Archer class is less useful here since Spirit Bow is the only way to damage Thorn

## Floor 5 (F5) - Livid

- **Requirement**: Catacombs Level 14
- **Boss**: Livid
- **Dungeon Size**: Medium
- **Boss HP**: 7M (Normal)
- **Special**: Requires Floor V completion for Shadow Assassin Armor

### Boss Mechanics
Livid spawns 8 clones from Endermen, each with a different color and unique attacks. Only the REAL Livid matters. The ceiling wool color indicates which Livid is real after the initial blindness wears off.

**Clone Types**:
- Vendetta Livid: Teleports + throws daggers
- Crossed Livid: Inflicts blindness
- Hockey Livid: Charging attacks
- Doctor Livid: Splash Potion of Harming (true damage)
- Frog Livid: Poison effects
- Smile Livid: Slowness potions
- Scream Livid: Knockback circle, true damage
- Purple Livid: HP leeching
- Arcade Livid: Bow attacks, erratic movement

**Critical Rule**: Hitting a fake Livid regenerates it to full HP AND heals nearby clones by 25%. Only attack the real one!

### Key Strategies
- Look at the ceiling wool color to identify the real Livid
- DO NOT hit fake Livids (they heal themselves and others)
- Focus all DPS on the real Livid only
- One phase fight; kill the real Livid and all clones despawn
- Tank class is very helpful for surviving all the clone attacks

### Notable Loot
- Livid Dagger (one of the best melee weapons in mid-game, guaranteed crit)
- Shadow Fury (teleporting sword)
- Shadow Assassin Armor set (top-tier mid-game armor)
- Last Breath (archer weapon)
- Warped Stone (teleportation item)
- Dark Orb

### Recommended Gear
- Adaptive Armor or Strong Dragon Armor (dungeonized)
- Juju Shortbow for archer class
- Spirit Sceptre for mage
- This is where gear starts mattering significantly

## Floor 6 (F6) - Sadan

- **Requirement**: Catacombs Level 19
- **Boss**: Sadan
- **Dungeon Size**: Medium
- **Boss HP**: 40M (Normal)

### Boss Mechanics
**Phase 1 - Terracotta Army** (2-minute timer):
Sadan animates 16 Terracotta mobs. Each kill reduces the timer by 1 second. Defeated Terracottas respawn after 10 seconds. Survive and kill until the timer (Sadan's Interest Meter) depletes.

**Phase 2 - Four Giants**:
Four unique giants drop from cages:
- Diamond Giant: "Sword of 1,000 Truths" for heavy damage
- L.A.S.R.: Eye beams with damage-over-time
- Bigfoot: Proximity-based stomp damage
- Jolly Pink Giant: Boulder throws with impact zones

Each giant has 25M HP.

**Phase 3 - The Giant One**:
Sadan merges all four giants into "The Giant One", combining all their abilities. A hole opens in the arena; falling in is instant death (even for Tanks). No knockback immunity on this boss.

### Key Strategies
- Phase 1: AoE clear Terracottas efficiently; don't stop killing
- Phase 2: Split the team to handle different giants simultaneously
- Phase 3: Stay away from the center hole. Focus DPS, avoid combined abilities
- Avoid waking Sleeping Golems (1 HP but transform into Woke Golems with 20M HP)
- Woke Golems can drop Ancient Rose

### Notable Loot
- Giant's Sword (extremely powerful, very rare)
- Necromancer Lord Armor set
- Sadan's Brooch
- Ancient Rose (from Woke Golems)

### Recommended Gear
- Shadow Assassin Armor (full set)
- Juju Shortbow (Archer) or Livid Dagger/Shadow Fury (Berserker)
- Spirit Sceptre or Midas Staff (Mage)
- F6 is a significant difficulty spike; undergeared players will struggle

## Floor 7 (F7) - Necron & The Wither Lords

- **Requirement**: Catacombs Level 24
- **Boss**: Necron (preceded by Maxor, Storm, and Goldor)
- **Dungeon Size**: Medium
- **Boss HP**: Necron has 1B HP (Normal)
- **Special**: This is the endgame normal-mode floor. Drops the best gear in the game.

### Phase 1 - Maxor
- **HP**: 100M (Normal)
- **Arena**: Factory-like setting with high ledges
- **Mechanic**: Maxor is invulnerable at first. Retrieve two Energy Crystals from ledges and place them on podiums to activate a laser beam. Lure Maxor into the laser to make him vulnerable.
- First laser: Maxor becomes vulnerable until 75% HP, then goes enraged and invulnerable again
- Second laser: No damage cap, finish him off
- **Minions**: Wither Guards (3.5M HP, ranged bow attacks) and Wither Miners (10M HP, melee)

### Phase 2 - Storm
- **HP**: 400M (Normal)
- **Arena**: Large hall with 4 diorite crushers, outer ring with control pads, lava rings
- **Mechanic**: Use control pads to activate 3 functional crushers (purple, green, yellow; red is broken). Crush Storm to stun him for 10 seconds of vulnerability.
- **Thunder Strike**: After a countdown, lightning strikes all unprotected areas dealing 2 bursts of 75% max HP as true damage. Stand under crushers for protection.
- **Enrage**: Triggered at 50% HP, significantly increases damage output
- Lava deals 20% max HP per hit

### Phase 3 - Goldor
- **HP**: 750M (Normal)
- **Arena**: Four sections with terminals, levers, and devices
- **Mechanic**: Goldor is INVULNERABLE while chasing players through terminal sections. Complete all terminals to progress; gates open after 5-second delays (or use Superboom TNT to skip).
- After all terminals are completed, Goldor becomes vulnerable and surrounded by swords
- Dealing damage to Goldor slows his chase
- Goldor never gets stunned, so consistent DPS is important
- **Attacks**: Greatsword throws, Frenzy attacks (extremely high damage)

### Phase 4 - Necron
- **HP**: 1B (Normal)
- **Arena**: Platform surrounded by lava
- **Opening**: Necron hovers players up then destroys the platform, dropping everyone into lava (20% max HP damage per hit)
- **Attacks**:
  - Wither Skull Barrage: Rapid projectiles from three heads
  - Frenzy Mode: Explosions centered on Necron; radius expands without damage, shrinks when damaged
  - Fireball attacks and Lightning strikes
  - Diamond Sword Summons: Four floating swords launched at players
  - Rotating Beams: Four horizontal beams rotating for 15 seconds, dealing 25% HP damage
- **Frenzy Triggers**: At 80% HP (teleports to center, blinds players) and at 5% HP (destroys outer ring)

### Key Strategies
- Phase 1: Assign crystal runners to grab Energy Crystals quickly. DPS Maxor during vulnerability windows.
- Phase 2: Coordinate crusher usage. Stay under crushers during Thunder Strike countdown. Wither Cloak Sword can protect from lightning.
- Phase 3: Have a fast player (usually Archer with high speed) do terminals. Others deal damage to slow Goldor.
- Phase 4: Stay close to Necron during Frenzy to damage him and shrink the explosion radius. Tank class is essential. Avoid lava.

### Notable Loot
- Necron's Armor set (Helmet, Chestplate, Leggings, Boots) - best all-around dungeon armor
- Maxor's Armor set (speed-focused)
- Storm's Armor set (intelligence/mage-focused)
- Goldor's Armor set (defense/tank-focused)
- Wither Helmet variants (Wither Goggles, etc.)
- Hyperion, Valkyrie, Astraea, Scylla (Wither Blade variants - best weapons)
- Handle, Wither Catalyst, Precursor Gear, Auto Recombobulator
- Implosion scroll, Wither Shield scroll, Shadow Warp scroll
- Necron's Handle is extremely rare and used to craft Hyperion/Valkyrie/Astraea/Scylla

### Recommended Gear
- 3/4 Shadow Assassin + Wither Goggles or full Shadow Assassin
- Juju Shortbow (Archer), Livid Dagger or Shadow Fury (Berserker)
- Spirit Sceptre or Midas Staff (Mage)
- Strong healing setup (Florid Zombie Sword, etc.)
- High Catacombs level (24+) with starred gear (5-star everything)

## Master Mode Overview

Master Mode is the endgame dungeon difficulty. All floors have massively increased mob HP, boss HP, and damage. Master Mode drops Master Stars and higher-quality item tiers.

### Master Mode Requirements
| Floor | Catacombs Level Required |
|-------|-------------------------|
| M1 | Catacombs 24 |
| M2 | Catacombs 26 |
| M3 | Catacombs 28 |
| M4 | Catacombs 30 |
| M5 | Catacombs 32 |
| M6 | Catacombs 34 |
| M7 | Catacombs 36 |

### What Changes in Master Mode
- All mob and boss HP massively increased (often 100x+ normal values)
- Boss damage dramatically higher
- Boss mechanics become stricter (faster revive timers, more minions, etc.)
- Item quality tiers 4-10 (vs normal mode tiers 1-7), giving higher base stats
- Master Stars can drop (applied to dungeon items for +5% stat bonus each, up to +25% on top of regular stars)
- With 5 regular stars (+50%) and 5 Master Stars (+25%), items reach +75% total stat boost

### Master Mode Floor-Specific Changes

**M1 (Bonzo)**:
- Bonzo HP: 200M per phase (vs 250k normal)
- Undead summons: 15M HP each
- Wither Skulls and Balloons deal 350k damage
- Drops: First Master Star

**M2 (Scarf)**:
- Scarf HP: 375M
- 2 of each Undead type spawn instead of 1
- Scarf has damage resistance based on alive minions (immune until 4+ are killed)
- Drops: Second Master Star

**M3 (The Professor)**:
- Professor HP: 600M, deals 150k damage
- Guardians revive every 7 seconds (vs 10) at 90% HP (vs 80%)
- Elder Rogue Guardians appear
- Drops: Third Master Star

**M4 (Thorn)**:
- Spirit Bear HP: 180M (vs 5M normal)
- Spirit Bow takes 6 hits to kill Thorn (vs 4)
- 30 Spirit Animals needed for Spirit Bear (vs 25)
- Drops: Fourth Master Star

**M5 (Livid)**:
- Livid HP: 600M
- All Livids deal more damage
- When any fake Livid dies, all remaining Livids get a damage boost
- Drops: Fifth Master Star

**M6 (Sadan)**:
- Sadan HP: 800M
- Terracotta HP: 250M each
- Mutant Giants spawn with 600M HP each, combining abilities of multiple giants
- After Phase 3, four Mutant Giants respawn alongside Sadan
- Drops: Overflux Capacitor, Giant's Sword (higher drop rate)

**M7 (Necron + Wither King)**:
- Necron HP: 1.4B with 2,100 defense
- Maxor HP: 800M, Storm HP: 1B with 1,200 defense, Goldor HP: 1.2B with 1,800 defense
- **Phase 5 - The Wither King** (Master Mode exclusive):
  - The Wither King cannot be damaged directly
  - 5 dragon statues must be destroyed by placing colored relics on matching cauldrons
  - 5 dragons spawn (1B HP each, 800k damage): Apex, Flame, Ice, Power, Soul
  - If all 5 dragons are alive simultaneously, the Wither King performs a team-wipe attack
  - Minions: Wither Husks (500M HP), Apostles (800M HP)
- Drops: Dark Claymore (0.07% from Bedrock chest, one of the rarest items), all Wither armor/weapons at highest quality tiers

## Dungeon Hub

The Dungeon Hub is accessed via a portal on Hub Island or through Fast Travel. Fall damage is disabled here.

### Key NPCs
- **Mort**: Queues players into dungeons via Party Finder
- **Ophelia**: Sells dungeon equipment and consumables
- **Malik**: Handles essence upgrades, dungeonizing items, reforging, and salvaging
- **Croesus**: Opens unclaimed dungeon reward chests (can claim missed chests from previous runs)
- **Adventurer Saul**: Gives solo dungeon access after F3 completion
- **Guildford**: Hub racing activities

### Features
- Queue for Catacombs and Kuudra
- 7 Fairy Souls to collect
- Hub Racing: Three routes (Crystal Core, Giant Mushroom, Precursor Ruins) with speed talisman rewards
- Party Finder for finding dungeon groups

## Frag Running

A popular money-making method where a solo player:
1. Enters F7 (or other high floors) alone
2. Rushes to the Blood Room as fast as possible
3. Kills The Watcher's resurrected giants for armor fragments
4. Leaves without completing the boss

This is efficient because fragments (like Necron fragments) sell for significant amounts and runs are very fast. Requires strong gear and high Catacombs level.
